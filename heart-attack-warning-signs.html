5 Heart Attack Warning Signs That Could Save Your Life

Critical information every adult should know - provided by medical professionals

Validated by Dr. Michael Rodriguez, MD
Board Certified Cardiologist
1

Chest Pain or Discomfort

Most heart attacks involve discomfort in the center of the chest that lasts more than a few minutes. It may feel like uncomfortable pressure, squeezing, fullness, or pain.

2

Upper Body Pain

Pain or discomfort in one or both arms, the back, neck, jaw, or stomach. This pain may come and go or be constant.

3

Shortness of Breath

This can occur with or without chest discomfort. You may feel like you can't catch your breath, even during light activity or at rest.

4

Cold Sweat, Nausea, or Lightheadedness

Breaking out in a cold sweat, feeling nauseous, or feeling lightheaded can be warning signs, especially when combined with other symptoms.

5

Unusual Fatigue

Sudden, extreme fatigue that is unusual for you, especially in women, can be a sign of heart problems.

EMERGENCY ACTION

If you experience these symptoms, don't wait - call emergency services immediately
